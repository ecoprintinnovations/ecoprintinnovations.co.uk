Frequently Asked Questions

Do you offer different solutions for different sectors?

Absolutely! We customise our approach based on your sector's specific needs. Educational projects focus on curriculum alignment and safety, while commercial projects emphasise durability and functionality. Arts and cultural projects prioritise aesthetic quality and creative expression.

What file formats do you accept for 3D printing?

We accept most common 3D file formats including STL, OBJ, 3MF, and STEP files. For educational institutions, we can also work with student-created files from Tinkercad and other beginner-friendly platforms, providing guidance on file optimisation.

How long does a typical 3D printing project take?

Project timelines vary based on complexity:

  • Small individual projects: 1-3 business days
  • Educational class sets: 1-2 weeks
  • Complex business prototypes: 2-4 weeks
  • Large museum installations: 4-8 weeks
We provide exact timelines after reviewing your project requirements.

Can you help with educational curriculum integration?

Yes! We specialise in helping schools and universities integrate 3D printing into their STEM curriculum. We provide lesson plans, project ideas, and training tailored to different age groups and educational levels. Our educational packages include age-appropriate design challenges and assessment rubrics.

What are your pricing structures for different sectors?

We offer tailored pricing:

  • Individuals: Pay-per-print with volume discounts
  • Businesses: Subscription models or project-based pricing
  • Schools: Educational discounts with bulk printing options
  • Museums/Cultural: Custom quotes for exhibition projects
Contact us for specific pricing based on your sector and needs.

What if I need both design and printing services?

We offer complete end-to-end solutions. Many of our clients start with just an idea, and we handle everything from concept development to final production. Our team will guide you through each step of the process, ensuring seamless integration between design and manufacturing phases.

Do you provide on-site training and maintenance?

Yes, we offer both on-site and remote services depending on your needs. For schools and businesses, we often conduct on-site training workshops. Maintenance and repair services are available both remotely and on-site within reasonable travel distance. We also offer annual maintenance contracts for institutions with multiple printers.

What safety standards do you follow for educational projects?

All our educational projects comply with:

  • Child-safe materials (non-toxic, BPA-free filaments)
  • UK education safety standards
  • CE-certified equipment recommendations
  • Classroom-appropriate design guidelines
We provide complete safety documentation for school administrators.

Can you replicate fragile museum artifacts?

Yes, we specialise in non-invasive 3D scanning and replication of delicate artifacts. Our museum services include:

  • Photogrammetry for fragile objects
  • Material matching for authentic reproductions
  • Scale adjustments for educational displays
  • Interactive component integration
We work closely with conservators to ensure artifact safety.

What post-processing options do you offer?

We provide comprehensive post-processing services tailored to your sector:

  • Sanding & Smoothing: For aesthetic finishes
  • Painting & Finishing: Especially for museum displays
  • Assembly: For multi-part business prototypes
  • Educational Kitting: Classroom-ready packages
  • Functional Testing: For commercial products
